# Formal Proposal to the Ministry of Education

**Date:** May 20, 2026

**To:** Honorable Minister of Education

**From:** Lead Community Maintainer, BOSC Community Library

**Subject:** Recommendation to Adopt BOSC Community Library for National Educational Resources

---

## Executive Summary

This proposal recommends the Ministry of Education adopt the **BOSC Community Library** as the standard platform for distributing open educational resources. Unlike proprietary alternatives, BOSC offers zero licensing fees, complete transparency, and freedom from vendor lock-in – saving taxpayer money while empowering local educational institutions.

---

## Total Cost of Ownership (TCO) Analysis

### 5-Year TCO Comparison

| Cost Category | Proprietary Solution | BOSC Open Source |
|---------------|---------------------|------------------|
| Licensing fees | $250,000 | $0 |
| Annual maintenance | $50,000 | $20,000 (optional support) |
| Customization | $100,000 (vendor-controlled) | $30,000 (in-house or local devs) |
| Training | $40,000 | $15,000 |
| Migration/exit fees | $80,000 (if switching vendors) | $0 |
| **5-Year Total** | **$520,000** | **$65,000** |

### Key Savings

- **87% lower total cost** over 5 years
- **No annual licensing renewals** – budget is predictable
- **No proprietary lock-in** – exit costs are zero

---

## Vendor Lock-in: The Hidden Cost

### Proprietary Software Risks

| Risk | Impact |
|------|--------|
| Price increases after adoption | Ministry becomes captive customer |
| Feature removal or unwanted changes | No control over roadmap |
| Data format incompatibility | Cannot migrate to other systems |
| Vendor bankruptcy or acquisition | Forced migration on vendor's timeline |

### BOSC Advantages

| Advantage | Benefit |
|-----------|---------|
| Open data formats | Always exportable |
| Multiple service providers | Competitive support market |
| Ministry can fork | Full control if community direction changes |
| No forced upgrades | Ministry chooses when to update |

## Additional Public-Sector Benefits

### Transparency
- **Source code is public** – Any citizen or auditor can inspect
- **No hidden costs** – Everything is visible
- **Community governance** – Decisions are documented and open

### Local Capacity Building
- **Local developers** can be hired to customize and support
- **Students** learn on the same platform used nationally
- **University partnerships** for ongoing development

### Security and Compliance
- **Self-auditable** – No black-box components
- **Fast patch response** – Community fixes critical bugs quickly
- **No backdoors** – Code is reviewed by global community

---

## Implementation Pathway

### Phase 1: Pilot (3 months)
- Deploy BOSC in 5 pilot schools
- Train local IT coordinators
- Gather feedback

### Phase 2: Rollout (6 months)
- Expand to all public schools
- Establish helpdesk and support
- Train teachers on resource use

### Phase 3: Sustainability (ongoing)
- Allocate 1 FTE for maintenance
- Contract local support vendors
- Contribute improvements back upstream

---

## Recommended Actions

1. **Approve BOSC** as the recommended open source solution
2. **Allocate $65,000** for 5-year TCO (vs $520,000 proprietary)
3. **Establish an OSS policy** requiring preference for open solutions
4. **Join the BOSC community** as a contributing member

---

## Conclusion

The BOSC Community Library delivers better educational outcomes at 87% lower cost, with zero vendor lock-in and complete transparency. No proprietary alternative can match this combination of fiscal responsibility and public accountability.

**We respectfully request the Ministry's approval to proceed.**
